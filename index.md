I'm interested in better understanding how social, political, and ecological forces shape our environment, and how our environment shapes us. I'm from Chicago, a raduating from the University of Chicago in 2021, using data-driven but conceptually informed research. 

Hello, and welcome to my portfolio! My name is Clyde, and I am a research and data analysis professional based in Chicago. I graduated from the University of Chicago in 2021 with a degree in Geography, and since then, I have been working in the field of social science research with a focus on spatial data.

Growing up in Chicago has had a profound influence on my interests and career goals. As a child, I was always fascinated by the diversity and complexity of the city, from its unique neighborhoods to its rich history and culture. I was particularly drawn to geography and the ways in which maps and spatial data could help us understand the world around us.

As I got older, I became increasingly interested in using data to better understand and affect the world. In high school, I participated in a community-based research project that looked at the impact of gun violence on local neighborhoods. This experience opened my eyes to the power of data analysis in driving social change and inspired me to pursue a career in research.

Since then, I have had the opportunity to work on a wide range of research projects, both in academic and applied settings. I have worked at Urban Labs, a research center focused on using data and evidence to address social challenges in cities, and I am currently a Research Data Analyst at RTI International, where I work on projects related to public health and healthcare.

In this portfolio, I am excited to share some of my best work samples, including research reports, data visualizations, and statistical analyses. I hope that these examples will showcase my skills and experience in research and data analysis, as well as my passion for using data to make a positive impact on society.

Chicago's urban development history has left a lasting impact on the city's landscape and people. The legacy of segregation, redlining, and immigration is still visible today in the form of uneven access to resources, disparities in wealth and health outcomes, and cultural enclaves that shape the city's identity.

As someone who grew up in Chicago, I was acutely aware of these issues and their effects on my community. I saw how certain neighborhoods were neglected by public services, how different groups of people lived in separate spheres, and how social and economic opportunities were distributed unequally across the city. I also saw how maps and data could help illuminate these patterns and tell a more comprehensive story about the city's history and present-day realities.

Studying geography and spatial data analysis allowed me to delve deeper into these issues and to understand the complex relationships between people, places, and power. By using maps and data, I was able to visualize and quantify the disparities that I had observed firsthand, and to explore potential solutions that could help create a more equitable and just city.

Overall, I believe that studying geography and spatial data analysis is not just an academic pursuit, but a way to engage with the world and make a positive impact on the communities we live in. I am grateful for the opportunities I have had to apply these skills to real-world problems, and I look forward to continuing to do so in my future work.

Thank you for visiting my portfolio, and please don't hesitate to contact me if you have any questions or would like to learn more about my work.


I've focused on developing strong skills in spatial analysis with R, including data wrangling, visualization, regression analysis, and causal inference, while emphasizing the significance of context and place in understanding the complex social dynamics that shape our lives.
